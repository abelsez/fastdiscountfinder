Phase I: The Adaptive Algorithm Decrypted

The SHL AMCAT is not a linear test. It uses Item Response Theory (IRT). The system starts with a medium-difficulty question. Your performance on the first 5-8 questions determines your "Stability Zone."

THE STABILITY ZONE LOGIC:
  • Correct on Hard: Ability estimate increases significantly.
  • Correct on Easy: Ability estimate remains stagnant.
  • Incorrect on Hard: Ability estimate drops slightly.
  • Incorrect on Easy: Ability estimate plummets.

STRATEGY: Never rush the first third of the test. If you are 100% accurate in Phase I, you can afford to guess in Phase III when time is low.

Phase II: Quantitative Warfare (High-Tier)

PATTERN: Compound Interest vs Simple Interest (Difference Logic)

Scenario: Find the difference between CI and SI on $10,000 at 10% for 2 years.

Formula for 2 years: $D = P(R/100)^2$
Calculation: $10000 * (10/100)^2 = 10000 * 0.01 = 100$.
Formula for 3 years: $D = P(R/100)^2 * (3 + R/100)$.

The Trap: They will give you the difference and ask for the Principal. Memorize these shortcuts to save 2 minutes of manual calculation.

PATTERN: Probability of "At Least One"

Scenario: Three people fire at a target. Probabilities are 1/2, 1/3, 1/4. What is the probability that the target is hit?

Logic: $P(\text{Hit}) = 1 - P(\text{None Hit})$
$P(\text{None}) = (1 - 1/2) * (1 - 1/3) * (1 - 1/4) = 1/2 * 2/3 * 3/4 = 1/4$.
$P(\text{Hit}) = 1 - 1/4 = 3/4$.

PhraseTranslation

Phase IV: MS Excel & Outlook (Technical Precision)

EXCEL: The Array Mastery

Question: How do you sum only values in Range A1:A10 that are greater than 50 and less than 100?

Answer: =SUMIFS(A1:A10, A1:A10, ">50", A1:A10, "<100")

The 90th Percentile Trap: They will offer SUMIF (singular). SUMIF only handles one condition. SUMIFS is for multiple. Don't be fooled by the 'S'.

OUTLOOK: Priority & Rules

Question: You want to automatically move emails from "Project X" to a specific folder. Where do you find this?

Answer: Home Tab -> Rules -> Create Rule. Or File -> Manage Rules & Alerts.

Phase V: Accounting & Finance (The Gold Standard)

RATIO ANALYSIS: The Efficiency Filter

Inventory Turnover: Cost of Goods Sold / Average Inventory.

High Ratio: Efficient sales, low storage costs. Low Ratio: Poor sales or "Dead Stock."

90th Percentile Logic: If Inventory Turnover increases but Sales remain the same, the company has reduced its average inventory levels (Lean Management).

THE GOLDEN RULES (RECAP)
  • Real Accounts (Assets): Debit what comes in, Credit what goes out.
  • Personal Accounts (Entities): Debit the receiver, Credit the giver.
  • Nominal Accounts (Expense/Income): Debit expenses/losses, Credit incomes/gains.
