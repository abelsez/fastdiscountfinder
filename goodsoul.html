MODULE 01: QUANTITATIVE APTITUDE (NUMERICAL)

Pattern: The Fibonacci Difference Q: Complete the series: 4, 5, 7, 11, 19, 35, ?

A: 67

The Trap: Most look for a linear increase. The Logic: Look at the differences: +1, +2, +4, +8, +16. The differences themselves are a geometric progression ($2^n$). The next difference is 32. Calculation: 35 + 32 = 67.
Pattern: Time & Work (Efficiency Variation) Q: A is thrice as efficient as B. Working together they finish a task in 24 days. How many days for A alone?

A: 32 Days

The Logic: Ratio of efficiency A:B is 3:1. Total efficiency = 4. Total work = Efficiency × Time = 4 × 24 = 96 units. Calculation: A’s time = Total Work / A’s efficiency = 96 / 3 = 32 days.

MODULE 02: LOGICAL REASONING (DEDUCTIVE)

Pattern: Syllogism (The "Some Not" Paradox) Statements: All Metals are Liquids. No Liquid is Gas. Conclusions: I. No Metal is Gas. II. Some Liquids are Metals.

A: Both I and II follow

The Logic: If all A are B, and no B is C, then A is trapped inside B and can never touch C. Therefore, No A is C. Also, if All A are B, then a portion of B is definitely A (Some B are A).
Pattern: Coding-Decoding (Reverse Alphabet) Q: If 'HUNTER' is coded as 'SFMGVI', how is 'GATHER' coded?

A: TZGSVI

The Logic: This uses Opposite Pairs (A=Z, B=Y, etc.). Rule: Position of letter + Position of code = 27. H(8) + S(19) = 27. G(7) + T(20) = 27.

MODULE 03: MS EXCEL & OUTLOOK (WORKPLACE TECH)

Pattern: Absolute vs Relative Referencing Q: You have the formula =A1*$B$1 in cell C1. You drag it down to C2. What is the formula in C2?

A: =A2*$B$1

The Logic: The $ symbol "locks" the reference. A1 is relative, so it changes to A2. $B$1 is absolute, so it stays fixed. AMCAT tests this to see if you can handle large datasets without breaking formulas.
Pattern: Outlook Scheduling Logic Q: How do you ensure a meeting only shows as "Tentative" on your calendar?

A: Accept as Tentative from the Invitation Email.

The Logic: SHL focuses on "Process Flow." If you simply view it, it stays "Unanswered." You must act on the invitation to change status.

MODULE 04: FINANCIAL ACCOUNTING

Pattern: The Error of Principle Q: A purchase of a Motor Van is debited to the "Purchases Account." What type of error is this?

A: Error of Principle

The Logic: A Motor Van is an Asset (Capital Expenditure). Purchases Account is for Goods (Revenue Expenditure). Treating Capital as Revenue violates accounting principles.
Pattern: Working Capital Impact Q: If a company pays its creditors in cash, what happens to the Working Capital?

A: No Change

The Logic: Working Capital = Current Assets - Current Liabilities. If you pay 1000 cash, CA decreases by 1000 and CL decreases by 1000. The net difference remains the same. Trap: People think "paying" means "losing" capital. It doesn't.
